EM

With EM, it is a multiplyer by the font-size of it's parent element. if our html/body has a base font size (usually 16px) 1em on its first child would equal 16px. 2em would be 32, etc. However, nested elements will continue to duplicate. So here, our more EM text is inside a div with 2em already. The first div will make it's font size (2em) equal to 32px, as it is double the parent font. The second will now be 2em or 2 times it's parent, in this case 2*32. So the font size of our nested div is now 64px.

Floating

Option two is to use floats. Floats will cause our divs to slide over in the direction we want until they come in contact with another float or the wall of their parent element. NOTE: this may require a clearfix, as floating elements will escape their parents containers. .parent { overflow: auto; } is a clearfix.
